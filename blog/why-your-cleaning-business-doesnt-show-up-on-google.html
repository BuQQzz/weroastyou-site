Why Your Cleaning Business Doesn't Show Up on Google (And How to Fix It)

By We Roast You  ·  April 16, 2026  ·  6 min read

Someone in your city just searched "house cleaning service near me." Google served up three results with photos, hundreds of reviews, and a booking link. If your cleaning business wasn't one of them, that job went to someone else. Not because they clean better. Because they showed up and you didn't.

Cleaning is one of the most competitive local service categories on Google. The market is fragmented, most operators are small, and the barrier to entry is low. That should work in your favor. Instead, it means whoever figured out Google first owns the top of the search results while everyone else fights over the scraps at the bottom.

Your Google Business Profile Is the Problem

The Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important piece of digital real estate for a cleaning company. It's what shows up in Google Maps, the local pack, and the sidebar on desktop. If your profile is weak, incomplete, or absent, you're not in the game.

Here's what a broken profile looks like: no photos, fewer than 20 reviews, a generic "Cleaning Service" category, no service list, no booking link, and a description that says "We provide quality cleaning services at affordable prices." Every single one of those is a signal to Google that you're not worth showing.

The profile test: Search your business name on Google Maps right now. If the first thing you see is a gray placeholder where your photo should be, someone else is getting your next customer.

You Don't Have Enough Reviews -- and the Ones You Have Are Old

Reviews are currency in the cleaning industry. Homeowners are letting a stranger into their house. They're reading reviews before they even look at your website. If your competitor has 150 reviews averaging 4.8 stars and you have 18 averaging 4.2, you lost before the customer ever clicked your name.

But it's not just volume. Google weights recency heavily. A profile with 200 reviews, all from 2021, ranks worse than one with 80 reviews spread consistently across the past year. If you stopped asking for reviews when things got busy, your ranking has been decaying ever since.

The fix isn't complicated. After every completed job, send a text with a direct link to your Google review page. Not an email. A text. Response rates are 5-8x higher. Set a goal of two new reviews per week and stick to it. In three months you'll look like a different business on Google.

Your Website Is Either Invisible or Irrelevant

Most cleaning company websites commit the same crime: they exist, but they don't say anything useful. No pricing range, no list of specific services, no city-specific pages, no before-and-after photos. Just a stock image of a woman in yellow gloves and a form nobody fills out.

Google's algorithm needs content to understand what you do and where you do it. If your site has one page with 200 words on it, Google has almost nothing to rank. Your competitor who published a dedicated page for "deep cleaning services in [city]" and another for "move-out cleaning in [city]" is showing up for those searches. You're not.

  • Write separate pages for each service type: standard cleaning, deep cleaning, move-in/move-out, commercial, Airbnb turnover
  • Write separate pages for each city or neighborhood you serve
  • Include real pricing ranges -- even a "starting at" number builds trust and improves time-on-page
  • Add photos from real jobs, not stock images

You're Invisible on the Search Terms That Actually Convert

Most cleaning companies think their target keyword is "house cleaning service." Wrong. The searches that turn into booked jobs are more specific: "deep cleaning service [city]," "move out cleaning [city]," "recurring maid service [neighborhood]," "cleaning company near [specific zip code]."

If you don't have content targeting these phrases, you don't rank for them. It's not about tricking Google. It's about giving Google something to show when a real person types exactly what they're looking for.

Quick win: Open Google and type "cleaning service near me" then look at the "People also search for" section at the bottom. Those are the actual phrases your potential customers are using. Write one page targeting each one.

Your Competitors Are Running Google Local Services Ads -- You Aren't

At the very top of local service searches, above the regular ads and above the map pack, Google shows Local Services Ads. These are the green "Google Guaranteed" listings. Cleaning services qualify. If you're not running them, your competitors who are appear before anyone even sees the map results.

Local Services Ads work on a pay-per-lead model. You set a weekly budget, Google shows your profile to relevant searchers, and you only pay when someone actually contacts you. For cleaning companies in mid-size markets, leads run $20-$50 each. If your average job is $150-$200 and customers return monthly, that math works.

No Niche Means No Differentiation

The cleaning companies that grow fastest aren't the ones trying to clean everything for everyone. They're the ones known for one thing: Airbnb turnovers, post-construction cleanup, commercial office cleaning, move-out deep cleans for property managers.

Specialization helps with Google ranking because niche searches have less competition. It also helps with conversion because a homeowner looking for an Airbnb turnover cleaner will hire the company that specifically mentions that service over the generic "we do it all" operation every time.

Pick your best niche, build content around it, get reviews that mention it, and rank for it. Then expand.

The Fast Version of What to Do

  1. Claim and complete your Google Business Profile -- every field, professional photos, full service list, booking link
  2. Start a review system now -- text every customer after every job with a direct review link
  3. Build service and city pages on your website -- one page per service type, one per city you serve
  4. Sign up for Google Local Services Ads -- the Google Guaranteed badge alone converts hesitant customers
  5. Pick a niche angle -- Airbnb, commercial, post-construction -- and own it in your content and reviews

None of this is complicated. It's just work that most cleaning companies skip because they're too busy cleaning. That's the gap your better-ranking competitors have exploited. Close it.
